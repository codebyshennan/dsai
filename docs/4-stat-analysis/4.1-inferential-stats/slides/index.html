Parameters Statistics (3/3)

  • Adequate sample size
  • Checking for sampling bias
  • Stratification when necessary
  • Standard deviation: Measures variability in the population
  • Standard error: Measures variability of the sample mean
  • For large enough sample sizes, the sampling distribution of the mean will be approximately normal
  • The mean of the sampling distribution equals the population mean
  • The standard deviation of the sampling distribution (standard error) equals σ/√n

Sampling Distributions (1/2)

  • Mean: $E(X̄) = μ$ (population mean)
  • Standard Error: $SE(X̄) = \frac{σ}{\sqrt{n}}$
  • Sample Distribution: The spread of values in ONE sample
  • Sampling Distribution: The spread of statistics from MANY samples
  • Standard Deviation: Spread of individual values
  • Standard Error: Spread of sample statistics
  • "Larger samples always give the right answer"
  • "Larger samples give more precise estimates"
